# Q4 — NotebookLM Experiment Log

## Tool Evaluated
Google NotebookLM (free version)
Evaluation Date: June 2026

## Scenario
ESG Sustainability Policy Briefing

## Documents Uploaded
1. Sustainability-Policy.pdf
2. esg-report.pdf
3. lecture-notes.pdf

## Experiment 1 — AI Summarisation
**Query:** Summarise the key ESG sustainability reporting standards
**Result:** NotebookLM accurately identified UN SDGs, SASB standards
and key reporting frameworks with inline source citations.
**Accuracy:** High — matched manual review of documents
**Time saved:** ~20 minutes of manual reading reduced to 15 seconds

## Experiment 2 — Source-Grounded Q&A
**Query:** What are the key governance requirements in the uploaded ESG documents?
**Result:** Returned detailed response referencing board-level oversight,
management responsibilities and ethical compliance frameworks
with numbered inline citations.
**Accuracy:** High — all answers traceable to source documents
**Hallucination observed:** None detected

## Experiment 3 — Governance Requirements Q&A
**Query:** What are the key governance requirements discussed?
**Result:** Identified board committees, ESG oversight structures
and Corporate Governance and Nominating Committee roles.
**Accuracy:** High

## Experiment 4 — Thematic Analysis
**Query:** Identify repeated sustainability themes from uploaded documents
**Result:** Identified Climate Change Mitigation, Stakeholder Accountability
and Regulatory Compliance as core themes.
**Note:** Tool correctly flagged that lecture-notes.pdf did not contain
sustainability themes — showing source awareness.

## Key Findings
- Source grounding significantly reduces hallucination risk
- Fast summarisation saves significant research time
- Cannot answer beyond uploaded documents
- Over-reliance risk identified — students may skip primary reading
- Data governance review needed before institutional deployment
